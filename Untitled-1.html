FAQs

What is Halo-halo?
Halo-Halo is a popular Filipino dessert, often enjoyed during the hot summer months but loved year-round. The name "halo-halo" literally means "mix-mix" in Filipino, which refers to the way you mix all the ingredients together before eating.
What are the ingredients in Halo-halo?
 made with a refreshing mix of shaved ice, evaporated milk, and a variety of sweet toppings. It typically includes sweetened fruits like jackfruit and banana, along with red mung beans, ube, leche flan, and chewy jellies such as nata de coco. For added texture, crispy rice flakes and tapioca pearls are included.
How much does a serving of Halo-halo cost?
The price of halo-halo can vary based on its size and the ingredients used. A standard small serving usually ranges from ₱80 to ₱90.
Can I get it with other toppings?
Some vendors provide extra toppings such as ice cream, langka, or chocolates. It's a good idea to ask about the available options.
